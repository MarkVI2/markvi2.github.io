Sessions

There are 3 major sessions that Enigma conducts regularly through out the month. These sessions are generally organised by the members of the Club itself. If you have an idea for a new session or want to take one yourself, get in touch with us on Discord.

The details for each session can be found below:

Competitive Coding Session
Mondays | 6 - 8pm | LT-2

The Competitive Coding session aims to take the programming skills of the members of the club to the next level. You may wonder how competitive programming problems help in doing so and argue that these problems do not relate to the real life programming work. For the most part, it is true. Then why do we do it? Because

  • Time limit always makes you write time efficient solutions
  • Critical test data helps you write correct solutions, in one go!
  • Further it makes you great at debugging code
  • Hard problems makes you break down the problem into chunks, solve them individually and bring it all together to solve the main problem

The sessions are conducted by Vihan and Vathsavi.

~

Machine Learning Session
Wednesdays | 6 - 8pm | LT-2

The Machine Learning session was started at Enigma to enlighten students with machine learning skills. This session generally takes place in two sections, one where the theory and mathematical concepts of the algorithms are discussed and the other where members get practical experience by working on datasets.

The sessions are conducted by Rochan and Raghav.

~

HackWeek
Once a month | 7-10 Days | Campus

HackWeek is an initiative by Enigma to foster the culture of exploring and devoloping solutions to problems in a short period of time. Unlike traditional Hackathons that are for a day or two, HackWeek as the name suggests is a week long event that comprises of a unique problem statement or domain given by a professor from the Computer Science department.

The event is organised by one of the faculty members and the club member.
